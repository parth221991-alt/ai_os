## The Core Insight

Every other formula teaches something or proves something. F11 makes something — for the viewer, about the viewer.

The AI output in F11 uses the viewer's own image, their city, their name, or their profession. The result is a visual that the viewer immediately wants for themselves. They don't need to be convinced of AI's utility. They need to say "I want that."

The Delhi/Mumbai travel poster is the canonical example: a cinematic double-exposure poster of a real person's face merged with their city's landmarks. "DELHI / A CITY. A CULTURE. A JOURNEY." This is not a productivity tool. It is a mirror.

**F11 only works when the output is beautiful enough to hold the screen for 10+ seconds without narration.** If the output isn't stunning, use F03 (Hollywood VFX) or F08 (Transformation) instead.

---

## When to Use F11

**Use when:**
- The AI workflow produces an output using the viewer's own photo, city, name, or personal data
- The output is visually impressive enough to fill the screen for 12 seconds without losing the viewer
- The aspiration is personal identity or lifestyle — not professional productivity
- You can identify a one-word personal identifier for the CTA (a city, a name, a profession)

**Never use when:**
- The output is average or merely competent — an average output kills this formula
- The workflow is too technical to demonstrate in 12 seconds
- The topic is career/productivity-oriented (use F01, F06, or F09 instead)
- You cannot show a real example generated from a real person's photo/data

## CTA Rules for F11

**Always Comment trigger. Never DM for this formula.**

Why: The comment-trigger mechanic is algorithmically superior here because:
1. Each comment is public — 1,000 comments saying "DELHI" is social proof that compounds
2. The word the viewer comments (their city, name) is a micro-commitment — they've declared their identity
3. Comment volume triggers the algorithm regardless of follower count
4. Other viewers see "DELHI" "MUMBAI" "PUNE" in the comments — this is evidence the community is active

**Trigger word requirements:**
- Must be personally identifiable (city name, first name, profession title)
- Never a generic word like "INFO," "PROMPT," "LINK," or "YES"
- The word is about the viewer — not the creator
- One word only

**On-camera CTA line (under 10 words):**
`"Comment karo '[TRIGGER]' — tumhara poster bhejta hun."`

**DM automation:** Comment triggers a DM with the exact ChatGPT prompt used in the demo.

## B8 — Visual Problem Open (Clip 1)

B8 is a new Scene Block created for F11. Its purpose is to show the "wrong" approach visually — before the creator's face appears.

**What it shows:** A real example of the current "before" state (a regular travel selfie, a generic LinkedIn photo, a plain certificate photo) displayed as a card or full-frame image.

**The visual metaphor overlay:** An animated element that marks the before-state as "wrong" — a 3D character with a prohibition circle, a greying/fade animation, an X-mark overlay. Available in CapCut's sticker/animation library.

**Duration:** 3–4 seconds. Fast setup. Not a scene.

**No creator face in this clip.** The creator enters in Clip 2 as the solution to the problem that Clip 1 established.

**Background for Clip 1:** Thematically relevant collage background (Environment E — Thematic). For travel: cork-board with travel maps, stamps, and photos. For career: messy desk or plain office setting. The background tells a story before the creator speaks.

## B-PROOF — Extended Output Reveal (Clip 4)

This is not a standard B7 Reaction clip. F11 treats the proof clip differently from every other formula.

**Duration:** 12–18 seconds. This is the longest clip in the reel.

**Display format:** The AI output fills the full 9:16 frame. No bezel. No container. No margins. The output IS the screen.

**Narration rule:** No narration during the first 5 seconds of this clip. Let the image carry the frame.

**Color grade drift:** Over the 12–18 second hold, the color grade shifts subtly (±5% warmth, ±3% saturation drift). This creates perceived motion on a static image at zero production cost. Begin at the natural color grade, warm slightly by midpoint, return to natural at end.

**Creator facecam:** Optional. If used: small BR (20–25%), positioned bottom-right or bottom-left, showing a reverential expression — not excitement, not explanation. The creator treats the output with the visual reverence of a painting being revealed.

**Proof Hold (Rule 19):** Music drops to 10% for the first 5 seconds. Returns to 25% after. No narration, no SFX during the first 5 seconds.

## B-SOCIAL — Platform Context Frame (Clip 5)

Shows the AI output inside the real platform where it would be posted.

**For Instagram:** The Instagram Reels upload interface — output loaded as the reel cover/media, the "Next ➜" button visible, audio suggestion showing.

**For LinkedIn:** The LinkedIn post composer with the image attached.

**What this clip communicates:** "This workflow is complete. The output is real. You can post this today."

**Duration:** 3–5 seconds.

**Camera:** Starts slightly pulled back (showing the platform interface context), then pushes in toward the output over 3–4 seconds.

**No narration needed.** The visual is self-evident.

## Formula Color Grade

**Clip 1 (B8 — Visual Problem):** Slight desaturation (-8%), slight cool shift (+3% blue). Visual signal: "this is the before."

**Clip 2 (B1 — Hook):** Full warm grade. Return to baseline.

**Clip 3 (B3 — Demo):** Full warm grade. Interface bright and readable.

**Clip 4 (B-PROOF — Extended Reveal):** Begin warm. Grade drifts: +5% warmth over first 8s, then -3% back toward neutral over final 7s. Subtle. Creates perceived life on static image.

**Clip 5 (B-SOCIAL):** Neutral grade. Platform UI should look exactly like the real app — no color treatment.

**Clip 6 (B5 — CTA):** Full warm grade. Eye-level static camera (VP13).

## Acting Directions

**Clip 2 — Hook (B1):** "Conspiratorial lean forward. This is 'I found something you need to see.' Not excited — more like reverent. Like showing someone a beautiful thing for the first time. Slight head tilt. Open palms facing up — an offering gesture."

**Clip 3 — Demo (B3):** "Informed, precise. Every action intentional. Speed is moderate — fast enough to feel efficient, slow enough to follow. No commentary face. The hands show the tool."

**Clip 6 — CTA (B5):** "Warm, direct, personal. 'I'm talking to one person.' Nod at the start. Shoulders relaxed. Slight smile. This is the 'I'll take care of this for you' energy. Eye level."
