## When to Use Each Formula

### F01 — AI Cheat Code
**Use when:** You've discovered a specific shortcut, hidden feature, or non-obvious use of an AI tool that most users don't know about.  
**Audience state:** Viewer already uses AI tools but doesn't know this particular capability.  
**Primary metric:** DM rate (list building).  
**Avoid when:** The feature is already widely known. If 3 major creators have already covered it, it's not a cheat code anymore.

### F02 — Future Shock
**Use when:** A real, traceable trend is creating urgency around an AI skill or knowledge gap. A deadline, a data point, a pattern of behavior you've observed.  
**Audience state:** Viewer is aware AI is important but doesn't feel the specific threat yet.  
**Primary metric:** Follow rate (audience building).  
**Avoid when:** You're working from vague predictions. Every claim in F02 must be verifiable. Use max 1× per week — overuse erodes credibility faster than any other formula.

### F03 — Hollywood VFX
**Use when:** You have a genuinely impressive AI-generated visual asset — a Google Flow clip, a Midjourney output, a Kling video — that could pass for professional production.  
**Audience state:** Viewer has low awareness of what current AI generative tools can produce.  
**Primary metric:** Save rate + Share rate.  
**Avoid when:** The output is average. A mediocre AI output with this formula does more damage than not posting. Only deploy when the output is genuinely impressive.

### F04 — Hidden Tool
**Use when:** You've found a tool (under 6 months of Indian audience awareness) that solves a real, specific problem better than what the audience currently uses.  
**Audience state:** Viewer is familiar with common tools but actively looking for better alternatives.  
**Primary metric:** DM rate (highest DM conversion of any formula).  
**Avoid when:** The tool is already mainstream in Indian tech circles. Do not showcase tools you haven't personally tested and verified.

### F05 — Career List
**Use when:** You have 3–5 verified, high-quality resources (certifications, tools, GitHub repos, newsletters) that genuinely accelerate someone's AI career or skill development.  
**Audience state:** Viewer wants to upskill in AI but is overwhelmed by options and doesn't know where to start.  
**Primary metric:** Save rate (reference content) + Follow rate.  
**Avoid when:** The list doesn't meet the quality bar. Do not list resources you haven't personally verified as valuable. Do not list more than 5 — use carousel for larger lists.

### F06 — AI vs Human
**Use when:** You can show a real, time-measured comparison between doing a specific task manually versus with AI — and the numbers are genuine.  
**Audience state:** Viewer does this task manually and either doesn't know AI can help, or knows but hasn't seen the time difference demonstrated.  
**Primary metric:** Share rate (highest of all formulas) + Save rate.  
**Avoid when:** The comparison is fake or exaggerated. The Indian tech audience tests these claims — if the numbers don't hold up, the credibility damage is permanent. Do not use if the manual method is genuinely comparable in quality.

### F07 — News Flash
**Use when:** A significant AI development has occurred in the last 48 hours and you can translate it into clear Hinglish with a specific Indian-relevance angle.  
**Audience state:** Viewer has seen the headline but doesn't understand what it means for them.  
**Primary metric:** Follow rate (news authority builds long-term audience).  
**Avoid when:** The news is older than 48 hours without acknowledging the delay. When you don't fully understand the topic well enough to explain it clearly. When the Indian relevance angle is genuinely absent or forced.

### F08 — Mind Blowing Transformation
**Use when:** You have a before-and-after that demonstrates a dramatic quality gap — a bad starting point transformed into an impressive output via AI.  
**Audience state:** Viewer has the same "bad version" of whatever you're transforming (a generic resume, a weak email, messy code) and can immediately see themselves in the before state.  
**Primary metric:** Save rate (prompt capture) + DM rate.  
**Avoid when:** The before isn't genuinely bad or the after isn't genuinely impressive. The contrast must be undeniable — anything less than striking doesn't warrant this formula.

### F09 — Three Step System
**Use when:** You have a complete, executable workflow that reduces a complex process to three clear steps — steps a viewer can run within 24 hours.  
**Audience state:** Viewer is doing this task inefficiently (or not at all) and is ready to upgrade their workflow.  
**Primary metric:** Save rate (highest alongside F06) — this is reference content.  
**Avoid when:** The process genuinely requires more than 3 steps to be useful (don't force it). When the steps require technical prerequisites most viewers won't have. When the system hasn't been personally tested and verified.

### F10 — Comment React
**Use when:** A real viewer comment (or common DM question) reveals a knowledge gap, raises a good question, or expresses a concern that represents what many viewers are thinking.  
**Audience state:** The commenter and everyone who thought the same thing but didn't ask.  
**Primary metric:** Comment rate (this formula's primary contribution is to the comment-engagement flywheel).  
**Avoid when:** Comment volume is too low to mine (under 500 followers — substitute DM framing). When the question is too complex for a 30-second answer. When featuring the comment would amplify negativity.

### F11 — Ego Output
**Use when:** The AI workflow produces a visually stunning output that uses the viewer's own photo, city, name, or personal identity — and the result is beautiful enough to hold the screen for 12+ seconds without losing the viewer.  
**Audience state:** Viewer wants something for themselves — not to learn a skill. The hook is "I want that" not "I should know this."  
**Primary metric:** Comment rate (Comment trigger = public social proof) + Share rate (viewer shares because the output is desirable to their own followers).  
**Avoid when:** The AI output is average or merely competent — a mediocre result destroys this formula. The topic is career/productivity-oriented. The workflow cannot be demonstrated in 12 seconds. No personal identifier exists for the trigger word.  
**CTA rule:** Always Comment trigger. Never DM. Trigger word must be a personal identifier (city, name, profession) — never a generic word like INFO or PROMPT.

## Content Selection Framework

### Step 1 — Identify What You Have

Before selecting a formula, identify which category of asset you have:

| Asset Type | Primary Formula | Backup Formula |
|---|---|---|
| AI tool shortcut you discovered | F01 | F04 |
| Completely new AI tool (under 6 months) | F04 | F01 |
| Impressive AI-generated visual/video | F03 | F08 |
| Before/after quality transformation | F08 | F06 |
| Time comparison (manual vs AI) | F06 | F08 |
| Curated list of resources (3-5 items) | F05 | F09 |
| Breaking AI news (under 48 hours) | F07 | F02 |
| AI trend or career threat | F02 | F07 |
| Complete workflow system (3 steps) | F09 | F05 |
| Real viewer comment to respond to | F10 | — |
| AI output using viewer's own photo/city/name | F11 | F03 |

### Step 2 — Apply the Quality Gate

For each formula, there is a minimum quality threshold. If the asset doesn't meet it, do not use that formula:

| Formula | Minimum Quality Gate |
|---|---|
| F01 | The shortcut must genuinely save time or effort — test it yourself first |
| F02 | Every claim must be traceable to a real data point or observable pattern |
| F03 | The AI output must be indistinguishable from professional production OR surprising in capability |
| F04 | The tool must be personally verified to work as advertised |
| F05 | Every resource must be personally evaluated as genuinely valuable |
| F06 | The time comparison must use REAL numbers — time it yourself |
| F07 | You must understand the topic well enough to explain it without simplifying incorrectly |
| F08 | The before must be genuinely bad; the after must be genuinely impressive |
| F09 | The system must be one you've personally run and verified works |
| F10 | The comment must represent a genuine gap that benefits the broader audience |

### Step 3 — Check the Weekly Balance

The formula library is designed for a weekly content mix. Check the current week's content against this balance:

**Target weekly mix (5–7 posts/week):**
- 2–3× tool/demo formulas: F01, F04, F08
- 1× career/education formula: F02 or F05
- 1–2× proof/comparison formulas: F03, F06, F08
- 1× news formula: F07
- 1× community formula: F09, F10

**Imbalance signals:**
- Too many F02 (Future Shock) → Audience is experiencing fear fatigue. Balance with F05 (career path) or F09 (system).
- Too many F01/F04 (tool formulas) → Channel looks like a tool aggregator. Balance with F09 (system) or F10 (community).
- No F10 in two weeks → Comment engagement will drop. Add a Comment React.
- No F07 in a week where news broke → Credibility gap as an AI news source. Cover the week's biggest development.

```

├── I found something new (tool, feature, trick)

├── I have a dramatic before/after
│   ├── Is the primary gap SPEED/TIME? → F06 (AI vs Human)

│   ├── Does the AI output use the viewer's own photo, city, or name? → F11 (Ego Output)

│
├── I have NEWS to share
│   ├── Is it breaking (under 48 hours)? → F07 (News Flash)
│   └── Is it a broader trend or career threat? → F02 (Future Shock)

│
└── I have a COMMUNITY moment
    └── Is it a comment or question from viewers? → F10 (Comment React)
```

## CTA Strategy by Formula

The formula library uses three CTA types. Understanding which CTA serves which goal:

| CTA Type | Goal | Formulas | Signal to Algorithm |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DM trigger ("TOOLS")** | List building | F01, F04, F03, F06 | DM = highest-intent follower |
| **Follow (@ai_snipp)** | Audience building | F02, F07, F05 | Follow = long-term subscriber |
| **Save** | Content virality | F06, F08, F09, F05 | Save = highest algorithm signal |
| **Comment** | Engagement rate | F10 | Comment = algorithm boost |

**CTA priority rule:** Never mix more than two CTA types in one reel. The primary CTA should be spoken in the video. The secondary can appear in the caption. A reel that asks for follow + save + DM + comment is asking for too much — the viewer does nothing.
