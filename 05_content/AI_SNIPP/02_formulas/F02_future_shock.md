# F02 — FUTURE SHOCK

**Formula ID:** F02  
**Type:** Timeline Fear + Action Path  
**Reel Duration:** 25–30 seconds  
**Production Complexity:** Medium — requires strong script, minimal UI demo  
**Repeat Frequency:** Medium — use 1× per week maximum (overuse kills credibility)  
**Primary CTA:** Follow (not DM — this formula builds audience, not list)  
**Derived from:** APIFY Week 1 theme "AWARENESS — You're not alone. Here's why this is hard." + competitor intelligence on timeline-fear content

---

## Purpose

Creates urgency around an AI skill or trend the audience is behind on. The formula doesn't scare — it informs with urgency and immediately provides the action path. The viewer finishes this reel feeling: "I was behind. Now I know what to do. This creator is the one who told me."

**Why it works:** The Hinglish tech audience has high FOMO about AI's pace. They feel behind but don't know specifically what to do. This formula names the exact thing they're missing and gives them the next step — which is to follow @ai_snipp.

**Psychological mechanism:** Fear of missing out → specific named threat → relief of clarity → follow for more.

**Warning:** This formula requires credibility. Do not use it for vague predictions. Every claim must be traceable to a real trend, statistic, or observable pattern.

---

## Best Use Cases

- AI replacing specific job functions (code review, content creation, customer support)
- Skills becoming obsolete in a defined timeline
- AI adoption statistics for Indian tech market
- Salary differentials between AI-literate vs non-AI-literate professionals
- New AI capability that changes the competitive landscape
- "The window to learn this is closing" scenarios

**Avoid for:** Generic "AI is the future" content. Vague predictions without specifics. Anything that can't be backed by a real observation.

## Hook Structures

**Hook Type A — The Deadline Hook (most powerful)**
```
"Agle 12 mahine mein, jo log AI nahi jaante, 
unke liye interview mein ek extra sawaal hoga 
jiska jawaab unke paas nahi hoga."
```

**Hook Type B — The Statistic Hook**
```
"[Stat]% Indian software engineers abhi bhi 
manually [X kaam] karte hain. 
Ye 2026 mein kaise lagega unhe?"
```

**Hook Type C — The Insider Observation Hook**
```
"Hiring managers mujhe bata rahe hain — 
candidates jo Claude nahi jaante, 
unhe shortlist hi nahi kar rahe ab."
```

**Hook Type D — The Direct Address Hook**
```
"Agar tum software engineer ho aur tumne 
abhi AI agents banana nahi seekha — 
baat karte hain."
```

**Selection rule:** Type A for broad career audiences. Type B when you have a real statistic. Type C when positioning as insider. Type D for developer-specific content.

---

## Story Structure

```
[0-3s]   HOOK     → The future state. Name the specific threat. One concrete scenario.
[3-8s]   AGITATE  → Right now, the gap. What the average person is doing vs what's required.
[8-18s]  INSIGHT  → What specifically needs to be learned/done. Name it. Show it briefly.
[18-25s] PROOF    → Evidence the threat is real. Stat, example, observable pattern.
[25-28s] CTA      → Follow for more. The "I'll keep you updated" promise.
```

**The PROOF beat is what separates this formula from fear-mongering.** Without proof, it's panic content. With proof, it's intelligence. The proof can be: a real stat, a screenshot of a job posting requiring AI skills, a before/after salary range, a competitor observation, or a real conversation.

---

## Scene Structure

| Clip | Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clip 1 | Block 1 — HOOK (URGENCY direction) | 8s | Full screen creator, timeline threat delivery |
| Clip 2 | Block 6 — STORYTELLING (SETUP) | 8s | Creator at eye level, describing the gap |
| Clip 3 | Block 2 — FACECAM or Block 1 (INSIGHT) | 8s | Creator + proof visual (stat card, job posting screenshot, skill gap visual) |
| Clip 4 | Block 5 — CTA | 5s | Warm close, Follow CTA |

**Optional Clip 0 (cold open):** 3 seconds of a dramatic statistic or text card BEFORE Clip 1. Works for this formula because it pre-loads the threat before the creator appears.

---

## Character Usage

**Clip 1:** Full screen, urgency direction. Creator is more upright than usual — this is not casual delivery. Eyes slightly wider, pace faster, leaning slightly more forward. But controlled — not alarmed. The character always sounds like someone who already processed this information and is now calmly delivering it.

**Clip 2:** Eye level (storytelling camera). The personal observation tone. "Main dekh raha hoon..." — first-person experience positioning.

**Clip 3:** Either full screen or facecam overlay if proof visual is on screen. Expression: serious-confident. The brow may furrow slightly when presenting the concerning data — genuine concern, not performance.

**Clip 4:** Warm CTA. The energy shifts completely from urgency to warmth. "Main tumhare saath hoon" — follow me and you won't miss the next one.

---

## Camera Language

| Clip | Camera Position | Movement | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | Slightly below eye (authority) | Slow push-in — starts at 0s, not 3s like usual | Earlier push-in = earlier urgency |
| Agitate/Storytelling | Eye level | Gentle push-in (2% over 8s) | Peer-to-peer truth-telling |
| Proof/Insight | Eye level or facecam | Static on facecam if proof visual shown | Let the data speak |
| CTA | Eye level | None — completely static | Follow promise delivered still |

---

## Editing Style

- **Opening seconds:** No music under the hook. Pure creator voice on silence. This is different from AI Cheat Code which also opens on silence but for a different reason — here it signals gravity, not playfulness.
- **Proof visual treatment:** When a statistic appears on screen, text should be large, white, clean. Not animated — placed. The stillness of the number makes it hit harder.
- **Background typography:** During the HOOK beat, large semi-transparent text can appear behind creator showing the timeline: "2026" or "12 MONTHS" — subject masking applied. This is the most effective use of background text in this formula.
- **Color grade:** Slightly cooler/more serious than standard content. Not dramatic — just 5-10% cooler temperature. Signals that this is not a fun hack reel.
- **Cuts:** Fewer pattern interrupts than AI Cheat Code. This formula earns attention through stakes, not visual novelty. One strong visual change per beat is enough.

---

## Audio Style

- **Music:** Starts under the AGITATE beat (not hook). Choose: tension-building cinematic bed, or a slightly more serious electronic instrumental. NOT upbeat. NOT hype.
- **SFX:** Minimal. A single impact sound when the proof statistic appears. Silence during the hook for maximum weight.
- **Pacing:** Creator speaks faster than normal during hook. Slows to normal pace during insight (information density is high — give the viewer time to absorb).

```
[THE PROMISE — why following solves this]

[HASHTAGS — Set B rotation (niche authority)]
```

**Caption rules:**


**Secondary — Save for Reference:**

## Flow Generation Strategy

**Key difference from F01:** This formula relies MORE on creator performance and LESS on tool UI. The clips are simpler to generate (no complex tool interfaces) but harder to get right emotionally — the urgency acting direction must be calibrated correctly.

**Clip 1:** Block 1 — HOOK SCENE. Acting direction: URGENCY (not SARCASTIC ROAST). Slightly tighter framing than standard (closer to face = more intensity). Camera starts push-in immediately. Add: "More upright posture than standard scenes. Spine straight. Controlled urgency — not panic."

**Clip 2:** Block 6 — STORYTELLING (SETUP beat). Eye level camera. Personal observation tone. Can include a slight off-camera gaze (memory/reflection direction from character_consistency_guide.md).

**Clip 3:** If proof is a text/stat card — Block 2 (FACECAM) with the statistic as the main content area. The main content area should be described as: "A clean, dark-mode infographic card showing [THE STATISTIC] in large bold white text. Minimal design. The number is the only element. Dark background, white text."

**Clip 4:** Block 5 — CTA. Eye level. Follow CTA delivery (warmer than usual — the urgency has resolved into a promise of ongoing guidance).

**Consistency risk:** Low for this formula. Full-screen creator clips are the easiest to generate consistently. The main risk is the acting direction drifting to "alarmed" instead of "controlled urgency" — add to prompt: "This is NOT panic. This is informed urgency. The character already knows what happens next and is calmly delivering the information."
