# F07 — NEWS FLASH

**Formula ID:** F07  
**Type:** Breaking AI News / Real-Time Translation  
**Reel Duration:** 20–28 seconds  
**Production Complexity:** Low-Medium — no tool demo required, strong script is the primary production asset  
**Repeat Frequency:** High — use 2–3× per week as news cycles allow  
**Primary CTA:** Follow (@ai_snipp) + Save for reference  
**Derived from:** APIFY Week 1 "AWARENESS" + Week 4 "ENGAGEMENT" themes + competitor analysis showing news translation content drives high follow rates among Indian tech audience

---

## Purpose

Translates a complex or high-stakes AI development into plain Hinglish for the non-researcher audience. When OpenAI releases a new model, when a major Indian company adopts AI, when a research paper changes the landscape — this formula is the vehicle for delivering that news in the AI_SNIPP voice.

The value is NOT the news itself — anyone can share news. The value is the translation: what this means for the Indian software engineer or student, stated in 25 seconds.

**Why it works:** The Indian tech audience consumes English-language AI news but struggles with technical density. @ai_snipp becomes the trusted intermediary — the person who reads it so they don't have to, and who tells them exactly what to care about.

**Psychological mechanism:** "I saw this headline but didn't understand it" → @ai_snipp explains it in 20 seconds → relief and comprehension → follow for the next one.

**Warning:** Do not cover news that is more than 48 hours old without acknowledging the delay. Freshness is this formula's primary asset. A News Flash posted 72 hours after an event is just a recap — different formula, different energy.

---

## Best Use Cases

- New AI model releases (GPT, Claude, Gemini, open-source)
- Major Indian company AI adoption announcements (Infosys, Wipro, TCS, Reliance)
- AI regulation updates (Government of India, EU AI Act, US executive orders)
- Research breakthroughs that change assumptions (AGI timeline shifts, new capability demonstrations)
- AI tool shutdowns, pricing changes, or major feature releases
- Indian tech market data (AI job postings, salary data, adoption statistics)
- Viral AI moments (a video, a demo, a tweet) that need explanation

**Avoid for:** News you haven't personally understood well enough to explain. News where the Indian-relevance angle is unclear. Speculative news without reliable sourcing.

## Hook Structures

**Hook Type A — The Breaking Announcement (most urgent)**
```
"Abhi-abhi khabar aayi hai —
[Company/event] ne kuch aisa kiya
jo sab kuch badal sakta hai.
Sun."
```

**Hook Type B — The "You Need to Know This" Hook**
```
"Aaj ek news aayi hai jo
har Indian software engineer ke liye relevant hai.
2 minute mein explain karta hoon."
```

**Hook Type C — The Opinion Hook**
```
"Meri honest opinion — 
[Company/event] ki aaj ki announcement
[positive/concerning/overrated] hai.
Reason suno."
```

**Hook Type D — The Relevance Localization Hook**
```
"Baaki log ye news padh rahe hain English mein.
Main tumhe bata raha hoon — 
tumhare liye iska matlab kya hai."
```

**Hook Type E — The Calm Amid Chaos Hook**
```
"Sab log Twitter pe panic mein hain.
Seedha bol raha hoon — kya hua, kyun hua,
aur tumhe actually kya karna chahiye."
```

**Selection rule:** Type A for genuine breaking news (first 12 hours). Type B for news that isn't viral yet but should matter to the audience. Type C when you have a contrarian or non-obvious opinion. Type D for news that seems India-irrelevant but actually isn't. Type E when a news item is causing overreaction.

## Story Structure

```
[0-3s]   HOOK        → Name the news. Create urgency or curiosity. Who/what/when.
[3-8s]   CONTEXT     → Why this news matters. The baseline everyone needs to understand it.
[8-18s]  EXPLANATION → What actually happened. Plain Hinglish. No jargon without translation.
[18-24s] IMPLICATION → For the Indian tech professional specifically. "Tumhare liye iska matlab..."
[24-28s] CTA         → "Follow karo — next update milegi wahan."
```

**The IMPLICATION beat is the entire value of this formula.** Generic news coverage stops at "here's what happened." AI_SNIPP News Flash continues to "here's what you specifically should do or think about this." The localization to Indian tech context is what separates this from a Reuters alert.

## Scene Structure

| Clip | Block | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clip 1 | Block 1 — HOOK (NEWS URGENCY) | 8s | Creator full screen, urgency delivery, news named |
| Clip 2 | Block 6 — STORYTELLING (SETUP) | 8s | Creator explains context, eye level, conversational |
| Clip 3 | Block 1 or 2 — IMPLICATION | 8s | Creator delivers the "so what" — full screen or with proof visual |
| Clip 4 | Block 5 — CTA | 5s | Follow CTA — warm but direct |

**Optional Clip 0 (news ticker cold open):** A 2-second text card showing a real headline or screenshot before the creator appears. Creates instant context. For maximum urgency.

**Alternative 2-clip structure (for very fast-moving news):**
- Clip 1: Hook + Context + Explanation (14s)
- Clip 2: Implication + CTA (8s)

---

## Character Usage

**Clip 1:** URGENCY direction (from prompt_blocks.md Block 1 — HOOK SCENE, modified). Creator more upright than usual. Pace faster. Eyes slightly wider — the "just read this" look. The character has processed the news and is ready to deliver it, not processing it in real-time. This distinction is important: the character is always the expert who already understands, not the confused viewer.

**Clip 2:** Storytelling camera. Eye level. Explanatory mode — the pace slows from the hook. The character shifts from "urgent delivery" to "let me break this down." Occasional brief off-camera glance signals "I'm thinking through how to explain this."

**Clip 3:** Either full screen or with a visual card (screenshot of the announcement, a stat card, a logo). Expression: informed seriousness. The character takes AI developments seriously — this is never "wow isn't this wild" energy, always "here's what this means and why it matters."

**Clip 4:** Warm follow CTA. The news urgency resolves into a promise: "I'll keep you updated. Follow so you don't miss the next one."

|---|---|---|---|

| CTA | Eye level | Static | Promise delivery — still and direct |

## Editing Style

- **Breaking news visual treatment:** For Clip 0 (if used) — a news-style text card. Dark background, white bold text, a colored "BREAKING" or "UPDATE" label in red or amber. Clean, journalistic aesthetic.
- **Creator lower-third:** On Clip 1, a small "@ai_snipp" watermark with a subtle "AI NEWS" label appears briefly (1.5s). Signals the content category.
- **Stat/headline cards:** If showing a specific stat or headline on screen, use clean text typography — no animation, no flashy motion. The data should look authoritative, not designed.
- **No timer elements:** This is not a time-compression formula. Timer graphics would confuse the category.
- **Cut pace:** Faster than most formulas. News content moves fast — the editing should match. Minimal visual holds, quick cuts between Clips 1-3.
- **Color grade:** Standard (@ai_snipp palette). Do NOT apply the cooler grade from F02 — News Flash has urgency but not existential threat. Neutral to slightly warm.

---

## Audio Style

- **Music:** Begins under the hook beat (not on silence like F02). Choose: subtle news-desk instrumental, restrained electronic bed. Low energy — the creator voice carries the urgency, the music is structural.
- **SFX:**
  - A single notification chime or subtle ping at the very start (signals incoming news)
  - Impact sound when the key implication lands (the "this is why this matters" moment)
  - No other SFX — news content should feel clean and credible, not produced
- **Pacing:** Creator's fastest delivery of non-hype content. The script should be tight — no filler words, no extended pauses. Every second has information.

## Caption Structure

```
[THE HEADLINE — first line, treat it like a news desk]
"[What happened] — [Why it matters in one clause]."

[THE CONTEXT — 2-3 lines of backstory]
[Brief explanation of who/what this involves]

[THE ACTUAL DEVELOPMENT — the news itself]
Kya hua:
→ [Key fact 1]
→ [Key fact 2]  
→ [Key fact 3 — the most important one]

[THE INDIAN ANGLE — localization]
Tumhare liye iska matlab:
→ [Direct implication for Indian tech professional]
→ [What to watch for / what to do]

[THE PROMISE]
Aise aur AI updates ke liye — follow karo @ai_snipp.
Roz kuch naya. Seedha point pe.

.
.
.
[HASHTAGS — Set C rotation (trending + news)]
#AINews #[CompanyName]AI #TechIndia #AIUpdate #ArtificialIntelligence
#SoftwareEngineer #AISnipp #TechNews #AITrends #FutureOfWork
```

**Caption rules:**
- First line is the news — not a "did you hear about..." opener
- Always include the Indian implication — even if indirect ("ye tool jab India mein aayega...")
- Use → for all news items (not ✅ — ✅ signals completed actions, → signals facts/developments)
- Follow CTA (not DM trigger — news formula builds audience, not list)
- Hashtags lean on news-adjacent terms: #AINews, #TechNews, #[CompanyName]

```

```

**Secondary — Save for Reference:**
```

```
"Comment mein batao —

## Flow Generation Strategy

**This formula is the least Flow-dependent formula in the library.** The creator voice and script are the primary production assets. Flow clips support the script — they are not the hook.

**Clip 1:** Block 1 — HOOK SCENE. Acting direction: NEWS URGENCY (similar to F02 URGENCY but faster, less grave). Key addition: "Creator delivers the news like someone who just read it and immediately came to the camera — the information is still fresh. Not alarmed. Informed. The energy of breaking something to a trusted colleague, not broadcasting to a stadium."

**Clip 2:** Block 6 — STORYTELLING (SETUP). Eye level, explanatory pace. Conversational, not scripted-feeling. The character looks like he's explaining this to one specific person, not a crowd.

**Clip 3:** Full screen creator OR Block 2 (FACECAM) if a proof visual is needed. If full screen: the acting direction is "informed conclusion delivery — this is the 'so what' the whole clip has been building to." If facecam: describe the main content area precisely — a screenshot of the actual announcement, a clean stat card, or a logo/product image.

**Clip 4:** Block 5 — CTA. Follow CTA. Standard warm close.

**Proof visual note:** Unlike F04/F05/F06 which have tool UIs as primary visual content, News Flash proof visuals are typically: screenshots of official announcements, clean stat cards (white text, dark background), or product/company logos. These are simpler to generate or source directly. If sourcing a real screenshot, do not use AI-generated text in screenshots — the audience will catch text errors in real company announcements.
