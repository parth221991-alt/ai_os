## BLOCK 1 — HOOK SCENE

**Use for:** Opening 0–8 seconds of any reel. The creator appears full screen, delivers the hook line, grabs attention.

**Formula compatibility:** All formulas. This is the universal opener.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
IDENTITY LOCK — ATTACH Character.png AS VISUAL REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

Indian male, mid-to-late 20s. Warm medium-brown skin, amber undertones.
HAIR: Tight natural coil curls, black, dense, medium length on top, short sides.
BEARD: Short full beard, ~5-10mm, clean edges, black.
GLASSES: Matte black rectangular frames. Always present. Brand identifier.
CLOTHING: Plain black V-neck t-shirt. Black lavalier mic clipped to chest. Dark watch on left wrist.
POSTURE: Natural upright, slight forward lean. Hands loosely interlocked or steepled (no finger-counting).

RULE: Face, hair curl pattern, beard, and glasses are LOCKED.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENE: HOOK — FULL SCREEN CREATOR

DURATION: 8 seconds. Vertical 9:16.

LAYOUT: Creator full screen. No overlays. No split content.

CAMERA: Medium close-up. Chest to top of head. Positioned slightly below eye level (gives authority). Shallow depth of field — creator sharp, background soft.

CAMERA MOVEMENT: Slow, subtle push-in. Barely perceptible zoom from ~1.00x to ~1.03x over 8 seconds. Increases intensity without distraction.

EYE CONTACT: Creator looks directly into the camera lens. Sustained. Confident. Does not look away during delivery.

ENVIRONMENT: Dark premium home studio. Background has charcoal tones with cool blue/teal glow from multiple monitor screens. Books on shelf partially visible. Overhead pendant light provides warm key light from above-front. "Build. Solve. Scale. Repeat." text visible in background (partially). High contrast: warm subject, cool background.

ACTING DIRECTION: [CHOOSE ONE]
  → SARCASTIC ROAST: Playful exasperation, light roasting. "Smart friend who's slightly disappointed in you but in a good way." Speaks fast. Natural.
  → URGENCY: Slightly leaning forward, tone of "you need to hear this right now." Eyes slightly wider.
  → INSIDER SECRET: Slight conspiratorial head tilt, lowered voice, like sharing something exclusive.
  → SHOCK VALUE: Neutral face → deliver hook → subtle reaction (raised brow, slight head shake at how good/bad something is).

GESTURE: Hands in natural resting position for first 2-3 seconds. Natural gestures during speech — open palm for emphasis, no finger counting, no pointing at camera.

SCRIPT (Hindi/Hinglish, deliver naturally):
"[INSERT HOOK LINE HERE]"

OUTPUT: High quality, high retention, vertical 9:16. Professional content creator aesthetic.
```

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENE: STORYTELLING — PERSONAL NARRATIVE

DURATION: 8 seconds per beat. Vertical 9:16.

STORY POSITION — [SPECIFY]:
  → SETUP (0-8s): "Before" — creator describes the problem/situation
  → TURNING POINT (8-16s): "Discovery/change" — creator reveals the AI solution
  → PAYOFF (16-24s): "After" — creator shows the result/transformation

CAMERA: Eye level for storytelling (peer-to-peer). Slight variation in framing across story beats to signal time passing — slightly wider in setup, tighter in turning point, medium in payoff.

ENVIRONMENT VARIATION FOR STORYTELLING:
  → SETUP beat: Can use slightly moodier, lower-energy lighting (darker, slightly less warm key)
  → PAYOFF beat: Brighter, more energetic — same studio but livelier
  → Transition: Environment energy should match story energy

BODY LANGUAGE: More expressive than standard content. Storytelling uses more natural gestures, more facial expression variation. The creator can look slightly off-camera occasionally (memory/reflection gesture) — but returns to camera for key revelations.

SCRIPT STRUCTURE:
"[SETUP LINE — describe the before state]
[TURNING POINT — the AI moment]
[PAYOFF — the result]"

ACTING DIRECTION: Natural conversational storytelling. Like explaining something to a friend over chai. The energy builds through the three phases. End on confidence, not excitement — "this is what happened and I'm not surprised anymore."

PACING NOTE: Storytelling scenes can breathe more than hook scenes. Short pauses are okay. They signal weight and authenticity.
```

## ASSEMBLY GUIDE — Building a Complete Reel

### Standard Reel Structure (4 clips × 8 seconds = 32 seconds)

| Clip | Block to Use | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clip 1 — Hook | Block 1: HOOK SCENE | 8s | Full screen, sarcastic/urgent opener |
| Clip 2 — Beat 1 | Block 2: FACECAM or Block 3: TOOL DEMO | 8s | First content point |
| Clip 3 — Beat 2 | Block 2: FACECAM or Block 6: STORYTELLING | 8s | Second content point |
| Clip 4 — CTA | Block 5: CTA SCENE | 5-8s | Warm close, follow/DM prompt |

### Bonus Clip Options
- Add Block 4 (NEWS) before Clip 1 as a 3-second urgency cold open
- Add Block 7 (REACTION) after Clip 2 as a response beat before Clip 3
- Split Clip 3 into Clip 3a (Beat 2) + Clip 3b (Reveal Reaction) for higher retention

### Transition Language Between Clips
When continuing from one clip to the next in Flow, always add to the next prompt:

```
CONTINUATION: This is Clip [N] of [TOTAL] in a single reel. The character is identical 
to previous clips — same face, same tight coil curls, same matte black rectangular glasses, 
same beard, same studio environment. Only the action, script, and [any variation] changes.
```
