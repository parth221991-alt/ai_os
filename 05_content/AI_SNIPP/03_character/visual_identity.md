## Color System

### Primary Palette (extracted from Character.png)

| Role | Color | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject warm key** | Amber/warm gold `#D4894A` | Main character lighting tone |
| **Deep background** | Near-black charcoal `#0D0D0D` to `#1A1A1A` | Studio background, negative space |
| **Monitor blue** | Electric blue/teal `#1E6FBF` to `#0FBCD4` | Background screen glow, data visuals |
| **Skin base** | Warm medium brown `#8B5A3C` approximate | Skin reference for AI generation |
| **Accent warm** | Burnt orange / copper `#B5622A` | Emphasis highlights, motion graphics |
| **Text primary** | Pure white `#FFFFFF` | Captions, main typography |
| **Text secondary** | Warm off-white `#F5E6D0` | Supporting text, lower-third captions |

### Color Rules
1. **Warm on subject, cool in background.** The subject is always lit with warm amber. The environment has cool blue-teal from screen glow. This contrast creates cinematic depth automatically.
2. **High contrast over low contrast.** Dark background + bright subject = premium look. Never a bright background.
3. **No neon, no RGB rainbow.** The palette is disciplined. Warm ambers, deep blues, and blacks only.
4. **UI overlays match environment.** When showing tool interfaces, use dark-mode UIs with blue accents. Light-mode UIs look out of place in this visual world.

## Primary Environment — The Dark Studio

This is the character's home environment. Use for: hook scenes, talking-head segments, CTA scenes, storytelling.

### Environment Specification

**Background elements (left to right):**
1. Bookshelf with books (slightly blurred, adds intellectual depth)
2. "Build. Solve. Scale. Repeat." wall text or framed sign — this is a BRAND ELEMENT, should appear when possible
3. Multiple monitors showing data visualizations, code, or analytics (creates technical credibility)
4. Small green plant on shelf (organic warmth against the tech environment)
5. Overhead pendant light (creates top-down warm key light)

**Depth:**
- Shallow depth of field. Background is recognizable but not sharp.
- Subject is always in crisp focus.
- Background blur creates separation and premium feel.

**Lighting breakdown:**
- Key light: Warm, slightly above and in front of subject (mimics overhead pendant + soft box)
- Background light: Cool blue/teal from monitor screens (natural for a tech setup)
- No harsh shadows on face — diffused key light
- Slight rim separation between subject and background

**Atmosphere:** Late-night work session energy. Productive, focused, slightly intense. This is where important things get built.

## Environment Variations

These are approved alternate environments for specific content types:

### Environment 2: News Flash Environment
**Use for:** AI news updates, breaking announcements, trend content  
**Description:** Same studio base, but with a news-ticker overlay or urgency-signaling color grade. Monitors show news headlines or stock-style data. Red accent lighting possible (urgency signal). More front-facing camera.

### Environment 3: Tool Demo Environment
**Use for:** Screen demonstration content where tool UI is the star  
**Description:** Creator at a desk, slightly further back. Dual-monitor setup visible. Mouse/keyboard present. Screen is primary. Creator is supporting element (facecam style).

### Environment 4: Minimal White / Clean Background
**Use for:** Carousel cover frames, thumbnail backgrounds, educational breakdowns  
**Description:** Clean white or very light gray background. High key lighting. Still uses same character identity. More "classroom" energy. Less cinematic.

### Environment 5: Outdoor / Casual
**Use for:** Trend reactions, casual commentary, "me walking and telling you this" content  
**Description:** Natural outdoor or neutral indoor (café, hallway). Handheld camera feel. More casual energy. Appropriate for conversational/reaction content.

## Camera Specification

### Default Camera Setup (Standard Scene)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Aspect ratio** | 9:16 vertical (Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts) |
| **Shot type** | Medium close-up (MCU) — chest to head visible |
| **Height** | Slightly below eye level — gives subject slight authority and dominance |
| **Distance** | Comfortable personal space — close enough to feel intimate, far enough to see gestures |
| **Depth of field** | Shallow — subject sharp, background recognizable but blurred |
| **Stabilization** | Slight camera movement is natural (slow push-in during speech) |

### Approved Camera Movements
- **Slow push-in:** Start slightly wider, slowly push toward subject during hook or key reveal. Creates intensity.
- **Subtle zoom-in:** 2–3% zoom over 8 seconds. Barely perceptible but adds engagement.
- **Static lock:** For CTA and information-heavy segments. No movement. Lets content breathe.

### Camera Angle Variations

| Angle | Use Case | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Slightly below eye level (default)** | Hook, talking head, CTA | Authority, confidence, direct engagement |
| **Eye level** | Conversational, storytelling | Peer-to-peer, warm, relatable |
| **Slightly above eye level** | "Let me show you something" reveal | Humble, teaching mode |
| **Close-up (face only)** | Emphasis on a key word/reaction | Emotional intensity, humor, shock |
| **Wide shot (torso visible)** | Gesture-heavy content, tool setup reveal | Energy, space for gestures |

### What to NEVER do with camera
- No fish-eye or ultra-wide distortion
- No horizontal (landscape) framing — always 9:16
- No tilted/dutch angle
- No extreme close-up (nose to chin only) — it's uncomfortable
- No extreme wide shot where face is small

## Typography System (On-Screen Text)

### Caption Typography (Primary)
- **Font family:** Bold sans-serif — Inter Bold, Montserrat ExtraBold, or equivalent
- **Style:** ALL CAPS for emphasis words, Sentence Case for normal captions
- **Color:** White `#FFFFFF` with black shadow or semi-transparent background for legibility
- **Position:** Lower 30% of frame (above safe zone minimum)
- **Animation:** Pop-in per word (not letter by letter, not slide — pop)
- **Size:** Large enough to read without squinting on a 6" mobile screen

### Background Typography (Statement Text)
- Used when character makes a key statement
- Large text (50–70% of frame width) placed BEHIND the subject
- Subject masking: text appears behind creator, not on top of face
- Creates cinematic depth
- Example: Character says "AI will replace you" — large text "AI WILL REPLACE YOU" appears behind him
- Color: White or amber, partially transparent (30–50% opacity)

### Lower Third / Brand Identification
- "@ai_snipp" watermark — always visible, bottom-left or bottom-right
- Small, not distracting
- Same font as caption system
- White text, slight shadow

### Typography DON'Ts
- No decorative/script fonts
- No rainbow gradient text
- No thick outlined cartoon-style text
- No Papyrus, Comic Sans, or any novelty font

## Motion Graphics Style

### Principles
- **Purposeful, not decorative.** Every graphic element reinforces what is being said.
- **Clean and flat.** No 3D extrusions, no skeuomorphic shadows.
- **Appear with the word/concept, exit cleanly.** Don't linger after their moment.

### Approved Elements
- Icon callouts (simple flat icons appearing next to relevant terms)
- Information cards (dark background, white text, minimal)
- Animated underlines / highlights on key words
- Tick marks (✓) appearing after each point
- Simple data bars / percentage visuals
- UI mockups of tools being discussed

### Prohibited Elements
- Cartoon explosions, stars, or clip-art
- Excessive particle effects
- Bright gradient overlays across the full frame
- Anything that covers the creator's face

## Sound Design Language

### SFX Palette (consistent across all content)
- **Transitions:** Whoosh, swipe, air cut — fast and clean
- **Emphasis hits:** Short impact sound when a key word lands
- **Reveal sounds:** Rising tone / riser when showing a tool or result
- **Tick/check:** Soft click when a checklist item is confirmed
- **Background texture:** Subtle cinematic tension bed under hook segments

### Music Style
- **Mood:** Confident, forward-moving. Not hype-bro, not lo-fi ambient.
- **Tempo:** Medium-fast. Keeps pace with speech.
- **Genre reference:** Modern cinematic instrumental, trap-influenced beat, or clean electronic
- **Volume:** Background level — speech is always primary. Music is felt, not heard.

### Audio DON'Ts
- No bollywood tracks (too regional, distracts from brand tone)
- No overcrowded SFX (every cut doesn't need a whoosh)
- No music that overshadows speech
- No jarring genre mismatches
