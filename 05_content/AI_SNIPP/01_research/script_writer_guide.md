# Script Writer Guide
## AI_SNIPP Production Intelligence

*This guide defines the scriptwriter standard for every @ai_snipp reel. It is not about what to say — it is about how to say it. The formulas define structure. This guide defines voice, energy, and the emotional mechanics that make viewers stop scrolling and take action.*

*The scriptwriter persona: You are a creator who has already lived the experience you're describing. You've already tried the tool, run the workflow, measured the result. You're not explaining a feature — you're reporting what happened when you used it.*

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE: DISCOVERY NARRATIVE OVER INSTRUCTION

Every reel script has a default failure mode: it becomes a tutorial. A tutorial tells the viewer what to do. A discovery narrative tells the viewer what you found. The difference is everything.

**Instructional (default failure):**
> "Open Claude.ai. Go to Custom Instructions. Paste this prompt. Save it. Now your responses improve."

**Discovery narrative (required):**
> "There's a setting in Claude that almost nobody uses. I added one line there — 27 words — and the quality of every response immediately changed. I'm going to show you what I typed."

Same information. The first version makes the viewer feel like a student. The second makes them feel like a detective who's about to get the clue.

**The mechanism:** Discovery narratives create a *promise gap* — the viewer knows something is coming and stays to collect it. Instructions are skippable because the viewer can imagine them. Discoveries are not skippable because the viewer cannot predict the specific revelation.

## SCRIPTWRITER RESEARCH — THE 3 QUESTIONS

Before writing any body beat, answer these three questions internally. Never skip them.

**Question 1: What is the viewer thinking right now?**
Map their skepticism level at the start of each beat:
- Beat 2 (post-hook): "Okay, interesting hook. Show me if this is real."
- Beat 3 (mid-reel): "I'm watching. This better not be obvious."  
- Beat 4 (CTA): "Alright, convinced. What do I do?"

Write each beat knowing exactly where the viewer's head is.

**Question 2: What is the one surprising thing in this beat?**
Not the feature. The *insight the feature enables*. The gap between what people expect and what actually happens.
- Wrong: "This tool has 3000 prompts pre-built"
- Right: "This tool has 3000 prompts pre-built — meaning you never start from scratch again — and most of them are better than what I'd write myself"

The surprising thing is the *implication*, not the *fact*.

**Question 3: What single line will the viewer quote to a friend tomorrow?**

This is the keystone line of the beat. It is specific, countable, or reversible conventional wisdom. Write the rest of the beat to build toward it. If you can't identify this line, rewrite the beat.

**Examples of keystone lines:**
- "This took 3 seconds. I've been doing the manual version for 18 months."
- "The free tier does this. No subscription needed."
- "I ran the same task 5 times. It beat manual every time."
- "Most tutorials teach you features. This one teaches you leverage."

## VOCABULARY — APPROVED & BANNED

### Approved: Interactive Language

These words and phrases create pull — they signal that something is coming and the viewer needs to stay to receive it.

| Function | Phrase Examples |
|---|---|
| Direct address | "your workflow", "watch what happens when you", "I'm showing you exactly", "before you do anything else" |
| Micro-reveal signal | "and this is where it gets interesting", "here's the part I didn't expect", "wait for the second line" |
| Discovery framing | "I was trying to do X when I found this", "I almost missed this completely", "buried inside the settings is" |
| Contrast setup | "Everyone does it this way. I tried this. The difference was immediate.", "The obvious move is X. The real move is Y." |
| Present-tense immediacy | "I'm opening this right now", "watch what loads", "see what it's doing" |
| Reaction invitation | "The result? I'll show you in one second.", "Keep watching — you'll want to see the output.", "And then this happened:" |

### Approved: Enthusiasm Markers

These phrases signal genuine excitement without sounding performative. Each must be attached to a specific, verifiable detail — not used generically.

| Marker | Correct Use | Wrong Use |
|---|---|---|
| Time compression | "This took 8 minutes. The manual version takes me 40." | "This is so much faster" |
| Insider signal | "I've used this in every client project for 6 months" | "This is really useful" |
| Payoff signal | "And this is the part that actually surprised me" | "And then something amazing happened" |
| Discovery confirm | "I couldn't believe this wasn't in any tutorial I'd seen" | "This is hidden" |
| Value assertion | "This one feature alone covers the cost of Pro" | "It's totally worth it" |
| Specificity anchor | "Not 'faster' — 8 minutes instead of 45. I timed it both ways." | "The results are incredible" |

### Banned: Instruction Mode Words

These trigger the "tutorial" frame in the viewer's brain — passive reception, scroll risk, disengagement.

| Banned Pattern | Replace With |
|---|---|
| "Go to X" as primary delivery | "I'm showing you what I opened — and why this specific screen matters" |
| "Click Y, then do Z" | "Watch what happens when I do this" |
| "Now you can do X" | "Here's what this means for your workflow" |
| "Step 1, Step 2, Step 3" as script content | Discovery framing + contrast + result |
| "This tool has [N] features" | "I found [N] things in this tool that changed how I work — here's the one that surprised me" |
| "Isn't that amazing?" | Specific reaction to specific detail |
| "I think you might like this" | "This changed how I work. Specifically [X]." |
| "You should try this" | "I've run this [N] times. Result is consistent." |
| "Let me show you how to" | "Watch what happens when I" |

## BEAT PATTERNS — REUSABLE WIRINGS BY BEAT POSITION

### Beat 1 — Hook (handled by hook_library.md)
The hook sets the energy ceiling for the entire reel. The body beats must maintain or exceed that energy. If the hook creates urgency, beat 2 must deliver the insight fast. If the hook creates curiosity, beat 2 must deepen it before resolving it.

**Energy bridge from hook to beat 2:**
The first line of beat 2 must connect directly to the hook's tension — it either *pays off* the hook's promise (for fast-reveal formulas) or *deepens* the curiosity (for multi-beat builds).
- Fast pay-off: "Here's exactly what I found." (F01, F04)
- Curiosity deepening: "And the reason most people miss this is because it's not where you'd look." (F09, F05)

---

### Beat 2 — Insight / Setup (typical: 8s)

**Goal:** Convert the viewer from "curious" to "actively interested." Deliver the surprising insight — not the obvious angle.

**Wiring A — The Reframe:**
```
[Common belief stated]
[Direct contradiction with evidence]
[Implication for the viewer]
```
Example:
```
Most people search for AI prompts on Google.
I found a platform with 3000 pre-written prompts — organized by use case.
I haven't opened a new search tab for this in 4 months.
```

**Wiring B — The Discovery Moment:**
```
[What I was doing when I found this]
[What I found — specific detail]
[What it meant immediately]
```
Example:
```
I was trying to speed up my content research when I found a hidden Perplexity feature.
It's buried inside Collections — most people never open it.
I set it up in 2 minutes. Now my research time is 12 minutes instead of 45.
```

**Wiring C — The Contrast:**
```
[The manual way — with a real time or effort number]
[The AI way — same specificity]
[The gap — stated simply, not dramatically]
```
Example:
```
Manual cold email draft — 25 minutes, decent.
I ran the same brief through this Claude prompt — 3 minutes.
I sent 40 emails that week. That's 880 minutes I didn't spend writing.
```

---

### Beat 3 — Proof / Demonstration (typical: 8–9s)

**Goal:** Show, don't tell. The viewer needs visual confirmation that this is real. The script supports the visual — it does not replace it.

**Wiring A — The Live Reveal:**
```
[What's on screen right now]
[What I'm doing / typing]
[What happens — specific result stated before it appears visually]
[Reaction beat — short, specific]
```
Example:
```
I'm pasting the exact prompt right now — it's on screen.
Watch what Claude returns in the next few seconds.
There it is — a full structured brief, with sourced data, ready to send.
I've run this same prompt on 3 different topics. Same quality every time.
```

**Wiring B — The Before/After Gap:**
```
[Before — what it looked like / how long it took]
[The input — what went in]
[After — what came out, with a specific quality signal]
[The gap stated once, plainly]
```
Example:
```
This was my manual version — took me 40 minutes.
I gave Claude the same brief and one constraint.
This came back in 8 minutes. Read both — the quality difference is on screen.
The gap isn't about speed. It's about the thinking it does between the lines.
```

**Wiring C — The Counter-Intuitive Proof:**
```
[What you'd expect to happen]
[What actually happened — the surprise]
[Why it matters for the viewer specifically]
```
Example:
```
You'd expect the AI output to sound generic.
This one didn't — I'll read the first line out loud.
The reason is the system prompt. I'm showing you exactly what's in it.
```

---

### Beat 4 — CTA (typical: 5–6s)

**Goal:** Remove friction. The viewer is already convinced — the CTA beat should feel like the obvious next move, not a sales pitch.

**Energy shift from beats 1–3:** The CTA beat drops in intensity intentionally (Rule VP13 — Eye Level Reset). The sarcasm, urgency, and proof energy step down. This is the "peer to peer" beat — the creator talking to one specific person.

**Wiring A — Resource Handoff:**
```
[The thing you're sending them]
[The exact action]
[One line that makes it feel easy / zero-risk]
```
Example:
```
I put the full prompt in a doc — the exact version on screen.
DM me 'PROMPT' and I'll send it directly.
It's free. Takes 10 seconds to set up.
```

**Wiring B — Follow for More:**
```
[What's coming next — specific, not vague]
[The follow action]
[The identity badge — who this is for]
```
Example:
```
I cover one Claude feature like this every week.
Follow AI Snipp so you don't miss the next one.
This is for people who use AI to actually build things.
```

**Wiring C — Save + Use:**
```
[Why they'll want to come back to this]
[The save action]
[One reinforcing detail that makes saving feel smart]
```
Example:
```
The 3-step system is on screen right now — all three prompts.
Save this — you'll want it next time you're stuck on a brief.
I've used it on 20 different topics. It works every time.
```

## FORMULA-SPECIFIC DISCOVERY WIRINGS

### F01 — AI Cheat Code
**Discovery frame:** You found something most Claude users have never seen. The "cheat code" framing means the discovery feels like an unfair advantage — not a feature.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: Contrast (what most people do vs what the cheat code does)
- Beat 3: Live demo of the cheat code in action — show the output gap
- CTA: "DM me 'PROMPT' / 'TOOLS' — the exact version is in there"

**Quotable line target:** "Most Claude users never open this setting. The ones who do get completely different output."

---

### F02 — Future Shock
**Discovery frame:** You've read the data others haven't yet. The urgency is earned — you're not manufacturing it.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: The data point + the Indian-specific implication (not the global stat — the local consequence)
- Beat 3: What this means for *this viewer's specific situation*
- CTA: "Follow — I post when this moves"

**Quotable line target:** "The timeline is [specific]. The people who act in the next [period] are in a different category than those who wait."

---

### F04 — Hidden Tool
**Discovery frame:** You found something before the algorithm found it. The "hidden" framing means the viewer gets credit for finding it via your channel — not via trending.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: What made you find it (the discovery moment) + why it's hidden (not on ProductHunt yet, buried in settings, no tutorial covers it)
- Beat 3: The 3 things it does — one per visual moment, each stated as a specific result not a feature name
- CTA: "DM me 'TOOLS' — I've been using this for X weeks"

**Quotable line target:** "[Tool name] does X — and the free tier includes it. No one's talking about this yet."

---

### F06 — AI vs Human
**Discovery frame:** You ran the test, clocked the times, and the result surprised even you.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: The human way — with a specific time and quality note (not "it took a while" — "it took me 45 minutes and I wasn't happy with the third paragraph")
- Beat 3: The AI way — same task, specific time, same quality standard applied
- Beat 4: The gap — stated as a decision point, not a declaration

**Quotable line target:** "I ran this 5 times. The gap was consistent. It's not a lucky result."

---

### F07 — News Flash
**Discovery frame:** You read the announcement and translated it for Indian users immediately. The value isn't the news — it's the implication.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: What happened — one sentence. No preamble.
- Beat 3: Kya matlab hai tumhare liye — the Indian-specific impact, stated as a concrete consequence
- CTA: "Follow — this is where I cover these moves first"

**Quotable line target:** "What this means for [Indian developers / freelancers / content creators] specifically is [concrete consequence]."

---

### F09 — Three Step System
**Discovery frame:** You built a system that removed your own dependency on manual work. You're reporting a solved problem.

Key beat pattern:
- Beat 2: Step 1 framed as a discovery ("the first thing I realized was that most people skip this step") 
- Beat 3: Steps 2 and 3 — each stated as a result, not an instruction ("Step 2 does the thing that used to take me an hour")
- CTA: "Save this — the full system is on screen and I use it weekly"

**Quotable line target:** "This 3-step workflow does in [X minutes] what used to take me [Y]. I've used it [N] times."

## SCRIPT ENERGY ARC — STANDARD REEL

The energy level across a 4-beat reel is not flat. It follows a wave:

```
Beat 1 (Hook):     ████████████ HIGH — urgency / roast / shock
Beat 2 (Insight):  ████████ MEDIUM-HIGH — revelation, building curiosity
Beat 3 (Proof):    ██████████ HIGH — visual confirmation, discovery payoff
Beat 4 (CTA):      ████ MEDIUM — warmth, peer-to-peer, friction removal
```

The CTA beat deliberately drops in energy — this is VP13 (Eye Level Reset). The drop creates contrast that makes the CTA feel genuine rather than performative.

If all 4 beats are at the same energy level, the reel feels flat and predictable. The dip before the CTA is structural — it creates the "human moment" that converts viewers into followers and DMs.

## SCRIPT RESEARCH SOURCES

When researching before scripting, prioritize these information types:

| Source Type | What to Extract |
|---|---|
| Personal experience | Specific times, specific outputs, specific moments of surprise — not impressions |
| Tool documentation | Hidden features, undocumented behaviors, non-obvious defaults |
| Competitor reels | What angles have been covered (avoid), what angles have not (prioritize) |
| Viewer comments | Real language the audience uses about the problem — quote this language in the script |
| Benchmark data | Specific numbers (time, quality score, cost) — never approximate |

**The personal proof floor:** If you can't answer "I ran this and here's what happened" for every claim in the script, the script cannot be produced. See Production Mode Failure Mode 2 (Personal Proof Gate).
