# CTA Library
## AI_SNIPP Research & Intelligence System

*A CTA is not an afterthought — it is the revenue event for an audience-building business. A reel without a clear CTA is a performance with no box office. This library contains every CTA pattern available to @ai_snipp, organized by type, formula, and intended outcome.*

*Rule: Every reel has exactly one primary CTA spoken on-camera and one secondary CTA in the caption. Never three. A viewer asked to follow + save + DM + comment does nothing.*

## DM CTAs

*DM CTAs convert viewers into high-intent contacts. The trigger word system enables automation (via Influish or ManyChat). The trigger word is always in quotes, always capitalized, always the only word the viewer needs to send.*

### Primary DM Trigger — "TOOLS"

The flagship DM trigger for @ai_snipp. Used when the promised deliverable is a curated list of AI tools, resources, or links.

**On-camera delivery:**
> 'DM karo "TOOLS" — main bhejtaa hun puri list. Free hai.'

**Caption version:**
> 'DM me "TOOLS" and I'll send you the full list 👇'

**Automation response template:**
> "Hey! Yeh raha tumhara AI Tools list: [LINK]. Aise hi tools ke liye @ai_snipp follow karo — roz naye tools share karta hun. 🔥"

**Best used with:** F04 (Hidden Tool), F01 (Cheat Code with tool as the vehicle)
**Lead magnet types:** Tool list Google Doc, Notion page with tool categories, curated link collection

---

### Secondary DM Triggers (Topic-Specific)

Use these when the promised deliverable is specific enough to warrant its own trigger word — not a generic "tools" list.

**"SYSTEM"** — For workflow or system templates
> 'DM karo "SYSTEM" — mera exact system template bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F09 (Three Step System), F01 (when the cheat code is a complete workflow)*

**"PROMPT"** — For specific prompt templates
> 'DM karo "PROMPT" — copy-paste ready prompt bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F08 (Transformation), F06 (AI vs Human — when prompt is the key asset)*

**"LIST"** — For curated resource lists (career, certifications, learning)
> 'DM karo "LIST" — puri list free mein bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F05 (Career List extended version), F04 (tool category list)*

**"FREE"** — For free tools or resources specifically
> 'DM karo "FREE" — yeh sab free mein milta hai, link bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F04 (when tool is free), F05 (free certifications list)*

**"CERTIFICATIONS"** — For certification resources
> 'DM karo "CERTIFICATIONS" — verified free AI certs ki list bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F05 (Career List — certification-specific)*

**"TEMPLATE"** — For document templates (resume, email, prompt chains)
> 'DM karo "TEMPLATE" — mera exact template copy-paste ke liye bhejtaa hun.'
> *Used with: F08, F06 (when the output asset is the deliverable)*

## SHARE CTAs

*Share CTAs are used as secondary CTAs only — never primary. Shares drive reach but don't build the DM list or follower count as efficiently as primary CTAs. Deploy share CTAs for content that has genuine peer-forwarding potential.*

### Share CTA Lines

**The gift framing:**
> "Yeh kisi dost ko bhej do jo aaj isse dhundh raha hoga."

**The "they need this" framing:**
> "Uss dost ko share karo jiske resume mein abhi bhi 'Proficient in MS Office' likha hai."

**The category share:**
> "Uss developer ko bhejo jo abhi bhi manually code review karta hai."
> "Uss student ko share karo jo AI seekhna chahta hai par shuru kahan se karein nahi pata."

**The WhatsApp angle (India-specific):**
> "WhatsApp pe bhej do — group mein kaam aayega."

**The "they'll thank you" framing:**
> "Share karo — yeh person tumhe baad mein thank karega."

---

### When to Use Share CTA as Secondary

Deploy share as secondary CTA when:
- The content has "pass-it-on" peer value (career tools, certifications — people forward to colleagues/friends)
- The reel directly names a relatable pain that a specific type of person experiences (developers, students, content creators)
- The proof or before/after is shocking enough to be naturally share-worthy (F06, F08, F03)

**Never use Share as primary CTA.** The viewer's primary action should always be one of the high-intent CTAs (DM, Follow, Save). Share is a downstream behavior from genuinely valuable content — prompt it but don't rely on it.

## CTA WRITING RULES

### What Makes a CTA Fail

1. **Too many CTAs** — "Follow, save, DM, and comment below!" → viewer does nothing
2. **Too vague** — "Let me know your thoughts!" → no specific action
3. **Too salesy** — "Don't miss out!" → feels like advertising, not conversation
4. **No deliverable** — DM CTA without a promised deliverable has no conversion incentive
5. **Wrong formula match** — Follow CTA on a save-worthy system reel wastes the moment
6. **Buried in caption** — CTA must be spoken on-camera; caption CTA is secondary reinforcement only

### CTA Quality Check

Before finalizing any CTA, verify:

- [ ] Is this the right CTA type for this formula? (check formula-CTA table above)
- [ ] Is there exactly one primary CTA in the video?
- [ ] Is the trigger word (for DM CTAs) specific enough? ("TOOLS" not "just message me")
- [ ] Is the deliverable promised in the CTA actually ready? (automation built, template prepared)
- [ ] Does the CTA match the energy of the reel's last 3 seconds? (warm for CTA clip, consistent with content)
- [ ] Is the caption CTA the secondary (not a competing primary)?
